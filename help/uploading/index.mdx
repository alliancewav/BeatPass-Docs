## Upload Overview

When you upload a track to BeatPass, several things happen automatically:

<Steps>
  <Step title="File Upload">
    Your audio file is securely uploaded to our servers.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Audio Fingerprinting">
    We create a unique fingerprint of your audio to detect duplicates and protect your content.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Metadata Extraction">
    We try to read title, artist, and other information from your file's tags.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Waveform Generation">
    A visual waveform is generated for the track player.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What You Can Upload

BeatPass supports the following audio formats:

| Format | Recommended For |
|--------|-----------------|
| **WAV** | Highest quality, no compression |
| **MP3** | Good quality, smaller file size |
| **FLAC** | Lossless compression |
| **AAC** | Modern compression format |
| **OGG** | Open format alternative |

<Tip>
  For best results, upload high-quality audio files (WAV or FLAC). These formats preserve the full audio quality of your productions.
</Tip>

## Track Information

When uploading a track, you'll fill out the following details:

### Required Fields

- **Track Name** — The title of your beat
- **Artists** — Your producer name (added automatically)
- **Key** — The musical key (C, D, E, etc.)
- **Scale** — Major or Minor
- **BPM** — Tempo in beats per minute (50-200)

### Optional Fields

- **Description** — Tell listeners about your beat
- **Genres** — Categorize your music (Hip Hop, Trap, R&B, etc.)
- **Tags** — Add searchable keywords
- **Album** — Add to an existing collection
- **Cover Art** — Custom artwork for your track

## After Uploading

Once your track is published:

- It appears in your producer profile
- Listeners can stream it on BeatPass
- Subscribers can download it with license certificates
- You can track plays and engagement in your Producer Dashboard
- You earn from the subscription revenue pool based on your contribution value
